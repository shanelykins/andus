Strategic Analysis

Andus North Star Metric Analysis

A comprehensive evaluation of potential North Star metrics for Andus's AI transformation consulting practice.

The Challenge

Andus helps companies "recompose" their business operating system for AI--transforming processes, workflows, people, and tooling. A North Star metric must capture the essence of successful transformation: if this number moves in the right direction, Andus is doing its job.

The ideal metric should:

Be measurable across diverse client organizations
Align incentives between Andus and clients
Tell a compelling story about AI transformation
Avoid perverse incentives (like celebrating layoffs)
Be simple enough to rally around

Metric Analysis

01

Revenue per Employee

Narrative Fit: Weak

Total company revenue divided by full-time equivalent employees. Tracked quarterly or annually, measured as change from baseline (pre-engagement).

Why It Works

  • Universally understood business metric
  • Directly ties to ROI and business outcomes
  • Easy to benchmark across industries
  • CFOs and boards already track this
  • Captures the "do more with less" promise of AI

Concerns

  • The human cost problem: As Mike noted, optimizing for this metric implicitly celebrates headcount reduction
  • Doesn't distinguish between healthy growth and unhealthy cost-cutting
  • Can be gamed through outsourcing or contractor classification
  • Lagging indicator--takes quarters to see movement
02

Decision Velocity

Narrative Fit: Strong

The average time from when a business question is raised to when a decision is made and communicated. Measured in hours or days, segmented by decision type.

Why It Works

  • Captures organizational agility--the core promise of AI transformation
  • Applies across all departments and functions
  • Positive framing--about enabling speed, not reducing people
  • AI's superpower is reducing information gathering and synthesis time

Concerns

  • Difficult to measure consistently
  • Risk of incentivizing hasty decisions over good ones
  • Requires instrumentation that most companies don't have
03

AI Utilization Rate

Narrative Fit: Moderate

Percentage of employees actively using AI tools weekly, weighted by depth of usage.

Why It Works

  • Directly measures adoption--the precondition for all other benefits
  • Leading indicator that predicts downstream outcomes
  • Easy to track with modern SaaS tools

Concerns

  • Usage doesn't equal value
  • Can be gamed by mandating tool usage
  • May plateau quickly
04

Meeting Time Reduction

Narrative Fit: Moderate-Strong

Average hours per employee spent in meetings, measured weekly. Also: percentage of collaboration that happens asynchronously.

Why It Works

  • Meetings are a universal pain point
  • AI excels at summarization and async handoffs
  • Easy to measure via calendar analysis

Concerns

  • Not all meetings are bad
  • Could incentivize isolation
  • Doesn't measure what people do with reclaimed time
05

Human OS Health Score

Narrative Fit: Very Strong

A proprietary composite score measuring organizational readiness across: process efficiency, tool adoption, workflow automation, employee capability, decision-making speed, and collaboration. Scored 0-100.

Why It Works

  • Proprietary = differentiation for Andus
  • Holistic view captures multiple facets
  • Creates ongoing engagement
  • Avoids single-metric gaming

Concerns

  • Black box risk--clients may not trust what they don't understand
  • Potentially seen as self-serving
  • Requires significant methodology R&D
06

Time to Value on AI Initiatives

Narrative Fit: Strong

Elapsed time from when an AI initiative is approved to when it delivers measurable business value. Measured in days or weeks.

Why It Works

  • Captures execution speed--a core consulting value prop
  • Outcome-focused: requires actual value delivery
  • Speaks directly to executive concerns about ROI timelines

Concerns

  • "Value" must be defined per initiative
  • May incentivize small wins over transformational projects
Recommended
07

Capacity Created (Hours Returned to Strategic Work)

Narrative Fit: Excellent

The number of hours per employee per week freed from routine/administrative tasks and available for strategic, creative, or high-judgment work.

Why It Works

  • Reframes the narrative: Instead of "we eliminated work," it's "we created capacity"
  • Positive for employees--they do more interesting work
  • Avoids the headcount reduction concern
  • Concrete and relatable: "You got 5 hours back this week"

Concerns

  • Requires defining "routine" vs. "strategic" work
  • Self-reported data may be unreliable
  • Freed time might not actually go to strategic work
Our Recommendation

Primary North Star: Capacity Created

(Hours Returned to Strategic Work)

This metric best resolves the tension Mike identified:

  1. 1 Sidesteps the human cost concern: Rather than celebrating fewer employees, it celebrates better work for the same employees. The narrative is about elevation, not elimination.
  2. 2 Is measurable and concrete: "We freed up 8 hours per employee per week" is tangible.
  3. 3 Aligns with Andus's positioning: "Recomposing the business operating system" is about redesigning how work gets done.
  4. 4 Creates a positive employee narrative: "AI took away my tedious work" is a story people want to tell.
  5. 5 Is defensible and differentiating: Requires Andus's methodology to measure--creating a moat.
Primary Metric

Capacity Created

We freed your people from routine work

Secondary Metric

Decision Velocity

And now they can act faster

Implementation Notes

To operationalize "Capacity Created":

1

Develop a work categorization framework

Define what constitutes routine/administrative vs. strategic work. Make it simple (3-4 categories max).

2

Create a lightweight measurement approach

Combine workflow tool data with periodic employee surveys (monthly pulse, quarterly deep-dive).

3

Establish baselines early

Measure before any intervention. The delta is the story.

4

Translate to business impact

Always be ready to convert hours to dollars for CFO conversations, but lead with the human story.

5

Build the benchmark database

Over time, Andus can say "best-in-class companies free up 10+ hours per employee per week."
