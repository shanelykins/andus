Executive Briefing

Open Questions &
Strategic Recommendations

Critical decision points that will shape Andus's go-to-market strategy and product roadmap

Following the strategy call with Mike — April 2026

Part I

Open Questions

These represent forks in the road worth discussing explicitly. Each question invites contemplation about a critical aspect of Andus's future direction.

01

What is the North Star metric for client success?

Why It Matters

Without a single anchor metric, every product conversation becomes diffuse. The pitch lacks a through-line, and clients can't easily articulate the ROI of working with Andus.

Possible Directions
→
Time-to-first-AI-win: Measure how quickly a client deploys their first production AI use case
→
AI adoption velocity: Track percentage of workforce actively using AI tools month-over-month
→
Decision quality improvement: Proxy through executive confidence scores or initiative completion rates
02

What does ongoing client engagement look like after onboarding?

Why It Matters

There's strong clarity on the front-end but less definition of what clients DO with Andus on day 91 and beyond. This affects retention, LTV, and whether Andus is a "project" or a "partnership."

Possible Directions
→
Subscription to Quorum: Ongoing strategic thinking tool with regular facilitated sessions
→
Managed AI operations: Andus runs "Agents as Staff" on behalf of clients
→
Quarterly diagnostic cycles: Regular re-assessment with new recommendations
03

Should the Simulator be entertainment, assessment, or both?

Why It Matters

The Sim is compelling as a hook, but its strategic value depends on what it produces. A game that's fun but doesn't generate insights is a marketing tool. A game that surfaces executive blind spots is a diagnostic instrument.

Possible Directions
→
Entertainment-first: Use it purely for engagement; diagnostics happen elsewhere
→
Assessment-embedded: Redesign scenarios to surface competency gaps while maintaining engagement
→
Tiered approach: Light version for events, deep version for paying clients with reports
04

What's the repeatable motion beyond ServiceNow?

Why It Matters

A lot of current energy is going into the ServiceNow event. But one marquee client doesn't make a business. The question is what's being learned that applies to the next 10 clients.

Possible Directions
→
Industry vertical play: Replicate the approach for other enterprise software companies
→
Executive cohort model: Run multi-company programs
→
Productize the engagement: Turn what's being built into a packaged offering
05

How does the product ecosystem fit together for a client?

Why It Matters

There are many compelling pieces but the client journey through them isn't obvious. Is there a sequence? Do clients pick and choose?

Possible Directions
→
Linear journey: Diagnostic → Simulator → Quorum → Product Factory
→
Hub and spoke: Diagnostic is the center; all other tools address gaps it identifies
→
Menu model: Clients choose based on maturity level and budget
06

What's the legal/ethical risk with Quorum's persona approach?

Why It Matters

Using 60+ AI personas based on scraped public figure data creates real exposure. Even if technically legal, a public figure objecting could create reputational damage.

Possible Directions
→
Shift to archetypes: "The Contrarian Strategist" instead of named individuals
→
Permission-based expansion: Only add new personas with explicit consent
→
Transparency defense: Be upfront that personas are AI interpretations
07

How should "Agents as Staff" factor into client business cases?

Why It Matters

Mike's insight that AI agents should be budgeted alongside human headcount is powerful but needs structure.

Possible Directions
→
Agent cost calculator: Tool to model agent deployment vs. hiring decisions
→
Org chart integration: Show what a "blended workforce" looks like
→
Case study approach: "This agent does 0.5 FTE at 10% of the cost"
08

What's the wedge product?

Why It Matters

Even with a full ecosystem, most clients will enter through one door. Which product is the easiest yes?

Possible Directions
→
Simulator as wedge: Low commitment, high engagement
→
Diagnostic as wedge: Positions Andus as assessors first
→
Product Factory as wedge: "Let us show you what's possible in 2 weeks"
09

Who is the actual buyer?

Why It Matters

"AI transformation" could be sold to CEOs, CTOs, CHROs, or strategy leads. Each has different budgets and decision timelines.

Possible Directions
→
CEO/Board level: Strategic imperative; longer sales cycle but bigger deals
→
CHRO/People Ops: Frame as workforce transformation; tie to L&D budgets
→
CTO/Innovation: Technical credibility matters most
10

What does Andus NOT do?

Why It Matters

The scope is ambitious. Defining boundaries helps focus resources and prevents overcommitment.

Possible Directions
→
No implementation: Andus diagnoses and designs; partners or clients build
→
No ongoing IT support: Andus transforms; clients operate
→
No generic training: Executives only; mass employee training is out of scope
Part II

Strategic Recommendations

Based on the gaps and opportunities surfaced in our conversation, here are concrete next steps worth considering. Each recommendation is designed to be actionable within the next 30 days.

1

Define the "Day 91 Problem" Before Scaling

The Recommendation

Before the ServiceNow event, document a clear answer to: "What does a client do with Andus 90 days after their initial engagement ends?"

Rationale

The current product suite is heavily weighted toward onboarding. Without a compelling ongoing value story, Andus risks becoming a one-time engagement rather than a long-term partnership.

Next Step

Map out 3 specific ongoing engagement models with pricing, then validate with 2-3 friendly clients or advisors before the event.

2

Make the Simulator Diagnostic

The Recommendation

Redesign the Simulator to generate a "readiness profile" for each participant, even if the primary experience remains game-like.

Rationale

This turns a marketing asset into a data-generating machine. Every executive who plays becomes a qualified lead with specific gaps Andus can address.

Next Step

Identify 5-7 specific competencies the Sim could assess, then work backward into scenario design that surfaces them.

3

Build the "Agents as Staff" Business Case Tool

The Recommendation

Create a simple calculator or framework that helps executives model AI agent costs alongside traditional headcount.

Rationale

This is a differentiated insight that makes Andus's thinking tangible. It creates a natural conversation about which agents Andus could deploy.

Next Step

Prototype a spreadsheet with 3-4 example agent roles, cost assumptions, and comparison to equivalent human roles. Test it in the next client conversation.

4

Migrate Quorum to Archetype-Based Personas

The Recommendation

Gradually shift from named public figures to archetype-based personas that capture the same strategic value without legal exposure.

Rationale

The current approach works but carries unnecessary risk. Archetypes can be just as effective ("The First-Principles Thinker" vs. "Elon Musk") and are fully defensible.

Next Step

Pick the 10 most-used personas, create archetype equivalents, and A/B test with a small group to validate that value isn't lost.

5

Use ServiceNow to Prototype the Repeatable Motion

The Recommendation

Treat the ServiceNow engagement explicitly as a pilot for a repeatable enterprise offering. Document what works, what doesn't, and what would need to change.

Rationale

The risk is building something bespoke that can't scale. By being intentional about learning, the ServiceNow effort becomes R&D for the broader business.

Next Step

Create a "ServiceNow Learnings" doc with sections for: what we'd do again, what we'd change, and what we now know about enterprise AI transformation buyers. Update weekly.

These questions and recommendations are meant as thought starters, not prescriptions. Happy to dig deeper on any of them.
